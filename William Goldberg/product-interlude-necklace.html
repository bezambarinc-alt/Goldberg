The Interlude Necklace

The Interlude family, carried to the throat.

The Interlude family, translated to the throat — the same articulated, flexible diamond line as the bracelet, extended around the neck. Worn, the necklace lies flat against the skin and follows the line of the collarbone rather than sitting on top of it.

Every link is hinged so the piece moves with the wearer without twisting or pulling. From the front, the eye reads a single continuous ribbon of diamonds with no visible clasp and no break in the line.

Completes the Interlude set — Hoops at the ear, Drops as their long companion, Bracelet at the wrist, and now Necklace at the throat. One family, four voices.
