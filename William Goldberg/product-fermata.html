The Fermata

A note held at the throat.

A single pear-shape diamond, bezel-set and suspended point-down at the base of the throat. The setting holds the stone close to the skin rather than letting it swing — a held note, not a passing one.

The bail and chain are deliberately minimal: a fine platinum line that disappears at the back of the neck and reappears at the front only as the curve of the bezel. From across a room, what the eye reads is the stone alone.

Worn open at the collar, the Fermata sits exactly at the point where the clavicles meet — and stays there, whatever the wearer does. It does not move. It holds.
