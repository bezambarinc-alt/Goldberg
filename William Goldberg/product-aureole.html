The Aureole

A ring of sunlight, worn at the ear.

A ring of fancy yellow pear-shape diamonds arranged point-outward around a hidden circular frame — a sunburst drawn in stones. Worn, the piece reads as a complete circle of light from the front, haloing the ear the way an aureole halos the head of a figure in old painting.

Every pear is individually claw-set so that each stone's point catches light on its own timing rather than in unison with its neighbours. In motion, the hoop appears to flicker — not blink, flicker — as though it were lit from within.

A statement piece for evenings that need no other jewellery.
