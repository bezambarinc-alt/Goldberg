The Alegro

The blue at the bottom of a deep sky, set in platinum.

The Alegro is built around one of the rarest stones the atelier has ever cut for a ring — a pear-shape fancy blue diamond, bezel-set and turned so that the point of the pear presses forward into the hand.

Around the centre, tapered baguettes radiate outward in a petal-halo, every baguette cut individually to meet the curve of the bezel at a perfect tangent. The effect, under light, is of the blue at the centre breathing light outward in white — a stone with an aura.

The shank splits into two parallel bands of channel-set tapered baguettes that flow down either side of the finger, meeting in a polished platinum bridge at the underside. This is not a ring that hides. Worn on the pinky, it sits low and heavy; worn across a table, it reads across the room.

A ring for the hand that does not need to draw attention — because the stone will.
