The Interlude Hoops

A pause between movements — in light.

A hoop is a closed line. An Interlude — the musical sense of the word — is a brief passage between two larger movements. The Interlude Hoops take both ideas literally: a continuous circle of diamonds, set to catch light on the inside as well as the outside of the ear.

Each hoop is pavé-set around its full circumference, with no joint visible from any angle. The inner edge is cut low to sit flush against the lobe; the outer edge rides proud so that the light it throws reaches the jaw.

Worn alone they are a quiet pause. Worn with any statement necklace, they are the breath between notes.
